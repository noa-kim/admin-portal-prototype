MCT2D Support for uACR

MCT2D is committed to helping participating practices succeed in improving uACR screening rates. During the 2025–2026 performance year, the Coordinating Center offers:

  • Patient-facing materials — handouts and clinic posters highlighting the importance of uACR testing
  • Individualized practice support — provided in partnership with the National Kidney Foundation of Michigan (NKFM)
  • Best practice sharing — presentations from high-performing sites at MCT2D regional meetings

Have an idea or suggestion? Contact the Coordinating Center at ccteam@mct2d.org

Why uACR Matters

MCT2D's new uACR screening measure focuses on a critical tool for the early detection of diabetic kidney disease, which affects nearly 1 in 3 adults with type 2 diabetes. Detecting elevated uACR levels enables earlier intervention, slowing progression to end-stage kidney disease and reducing cardiovascular risk.

How MCT2D's measure compares to HEDIS
The MCT2D uACR measure closely aligns with the HEDIS Kidney Health Evaluation definition, with two differences: it focuses exclusively on adults with type 2 diabetes, and it does not include eGFR at this time (due to inconsistent eGFR reporting across data sources). This alignment reduces burden for practices participating in multiple programs.

Eligible Population (Denominator)

For all BCBSM/BCN/BCBS-MA/BCN-A patients attributed to an MCT2D participating primary care physician who are:

  • Aged ≥18 and ≤75 (patients turn 76, they drop out of the measure)
  • Meet the MCT2D criteria for inclusion in the data registry
  • Have had at least one A1c record (clinical or claims) between the start of the lookback period (Jun 1, 2024) and the end of the performance period (Apr 30, 2026)

Patients who are 75 years old will remain in the measure until they turn 76. During the two-month locked period, patients who will turn 76 before Apr 30, 2026 are proactively dropped.

Eligible Physicians

All physicians at MCT2D participating primary care practices deemed eligible for VBR by BCBSM are eligible for the A1c Year 2 performance measure VBR.

Measurement Level

Practice-level measurement. All primary care practices, regardless of the size of their patient population, are measured at the practice level.

Measurement Population Definition

The percent of members aged ≥18 and ≤75 with type 2 diabetes whose most recent glycemic status (A1c) was at the following level during the measurement year:

  • A1c <8.0% for commercial insurance
  • A1c ≤9.0% for Medicare insurance

Commercial vs. Medicare distinction is made by whether a patient is 65 years of age or older, or via a specific indicator from BCBSM/BCN indicating Medicare enrollment.

Goal Rate

MCT2D aligns with the HEDIS 90th percentile rate:

  • 74.45% of patients with A1c <8% for the commercial population
  • 91% of patients with A1c ≤9% for the Medicare population
Data

MCT2D uses dashboards developed by the Michigan Data Collaborative, drawing on A1c rates from both clinical (PPQC) and claims data.

The denominator is not locked until the final 2 months. It remains open to change until Feb 28, 2026, at which point it is held steady — no new patients are added for the final 2 months (Mar 1 – Apr 30, 2026). Because the denominator is not locked, MCT2D will not provide baseline patient lists.

FAQs
Why is MCT2D removing aggregate groups and measuring each practice individually?
While aggregation can be valuable, it introduces administrative and analytical challenges — particularly during organizational changes like PO and practice merges, provider migration, and retirements. Instability of patient populations means re-aggregation undermines consistency over time. Additionally, aggregation creates interdependence: a single underperforming practice can lower the group's overall score. Measuring practices individually maintains transparency, stability, and meaningful practice-level insight. MCT2D will evaluate smaller practices at the end of the cycle and revisit aggregation in the future.
Why doesn't my practice-level patient count match the sum of individual provider attribution counts?
Patients may be attributed to multiple providers within a practice. In provider-level counts, one patient may appear under two providers. For practice-level performance, a patient is counted only once — so the practice total may be lower than the sum of individual provider counts.

New for 2025–2026
This is a brand-new performance measure for primary care practices. Urine albumin-creatinine ratio (uACR) screening is an evidence-based approach to detecting early kidney disease in patients with type 2 diabetes.
Eligible Population (Denominator)

For all BCBSM/BCN/BCBS-MA/BCN-A patients attributed to an MCT2D participating primary care practice who are:

  • Aged 18–85
  • Meet MCT2D criteria for inclusion in the data registry
  • Have at least one claim (for any service) since the baseline start (Jan 1, 2024) through the population lock date (May 31, 2026), as received by MCT2D
Population Lock
The denominator is open to change until May 31, 2026, at which point it is held steady. No new patients are added for the final 3 months. A patient list will not be provided after the denominator is locked, as MCT2D will not receive the 5/31/2026 data update until after the end of the measurement period.
Reward Amount

Combined scorecard: 100 points total (participation + performance). Specific VBR point thresholds for this year to be available by early June 2025.

Eligible Physicians

Any physicians deemed VBR-eligible by BCBSM are eligible. BCBSM finalizes the eligible physician list on Sep 1, 2025, shared via the MCT2D Admin Portal.

Measurement Level

Practice-level measurement. All primary care practices are measured at the practice level, regardless of patient population size.

Measurement Population Definition (Numerator)

Patients meeting the eligibility criteria who have a urine albumin-creatinine ratio (uACR) record during the measurement year (Jul 1, 2025 – Aug 31, 2026).

Data sources: clinical (PPQC) and BCBSM/BCN medical claims data.

Goal Rate

Practices must achieve a 10% absolute improvement over their baseline rate (e.g., baseline 54.5% → goal 64.5%), up to a maximum of 90%.

  • Baseline 80%–90%: only need to improve to 90%
  • Baseline >90%: must maintain at least 90% during the measurement year

Baseline period: Jan 1, 2024 – Feb 28, 2025 (based on April 2025 data refresh).

Data & Intervention Period

Intervention period: Jul 1, 2025 – Aug 31, 2026 (14 months)

uACR values collected between Mar 1, 2025 – Jun 30, 2025 do not count toward meeting the measure. The 14-month intervention period ensures clinical appropriateness for repeat annual testing.

Both a list of patients who have received a uACR screening and a list of those who have not can be identified using filters on the Renal Details page of the MCT2D Patient Data Dashboard.

Additional Requirements
  • The PO must log into the MCT2D Patient Data Dashboard on a quarterly basis and disseminate information to their practices. Verified via Michigan Data Collaborative login/usage records.
  • Practices with a baseline rate >90% are required to present at the MCT2D fall regional meeting to share their approach to uACR testing.
FAQs
Why is MCT2D not aligning with HEDIS and including the eGFR component?
eGFR data has not been consistently reported across MCT2D data sources, which would have made performance tracking across practices more difficult and less equitable.
Will this measure be shown on the MCT2D Patient Data Dashboard?
Yes. uACR screening information is included on the Summary Measures page, beginning with the October dashboard enhancement (data through Jul 31, 2025).

Key CPT Code: 95251
This measure specifically tracks CGM data interpretation (CPT 95251), not simply having a CGM device. Eligible patients are those already on a CGM who need annual data review documented via 95251 billing.
Eligible Population (Denominator)

For all BCBSM/BCN/BCBS-MA/BCN-A patients attributed to an MCT2D participating endocrinology practice who are:

  • Aged 18 and above
  • Meet MCT2D criteria for inclusion in the data registry
  • Have had at least one claim for a CGM device within the 12-month intervention period (including CPT 95251)

See appendix for the full list of CGM device codes (CPT, HCPCS, and NDC pharmacy claims) used to determine CGM device use.

Eligible Physicians

Any endocrinologist who is part of a continuing practice (started in MCT2D prior to 2025) and is deemed VBR-eligible by BCBSM.

Measurement Level

Practice-level measurement. All continuing endocrinology practices, regardless of patient population size, are measured at the practice level.

Measurement Population Definition (Numerator)

The percent of eligible patients (on a CGM device, per above) who have had at least one CPT 95251 billing claim within the 14-month measurement period (Jul 1, 2025 – Aug 31, 2026).

Goal Rate

Practices must achieve a 5% absolute improvement over baseline rate (e.g., baseline 54.5% → goal 59.5%), up to the collaborative-wide 95th percentile benchmark of 89.59%.

Practices already above 89.59% must maintain performance throughout the measurement period.

Baseline measurement periods:

  • CGM device claims (denominator): Jan 1, 2024 – Dec 31, 2024
  • 95251 billing claims (numerator): Jan 1, 2024 – Feb 28, 2025

Final baseline data based on April 2025 data refresh.

Claims collected between Mar 1, 2025 – Jun 30, 2025 do not count toward the measure.
